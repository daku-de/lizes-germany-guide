SCHLOSS NEUSCHWANSTEIN
[노이슈완슈타인]
GENERAL

While this castle might look older than it is, it was only built in 1884! Nevertheless it is a beautiful castle near the German Alps. That's probably the same Walt Disney thought, when he visited here, and decided that it has great fairytale potential.

It's one of the most touristy places in Germany, so you should decide wisely when you want to visit here. Especially in the summer months, it is very crowded. And it makes sense to visit the castle early in the day so there are way less people!

The good thing is that you can go to the castle without buying any ticket! You can also take a horse-drawn carriage to go from the village up to the castle.

LOCATION
RECOMMENDED ACTIVITIES

Take a hike along one of the many hiking trails and get a view on the castle without many tourists.

How about a photo session on Mary's bridge with the picturesque castle and valley in the background?

Take a tour inside the castle and get an impression of the beautiful romantic architecture.

EUROPAPARK really is an awesome theme park with many awesome attractions. I went here many times with my family when I was younger. And every single time I was impressed afterwards. It is themed around different European countries, so it really is a perfect spot to visit on a Europe trip.

There is a whole resort in front of the park with many awesome themed hotels and even a themed camping site! Hotel guests also have advantages like an early park entry. Which is really awesome because you can experience a popular attraction with almost no waiting time! I highly recommend staying there and visiting the park for at least 2 days. There is so so much to do.

I know you think you can't ride rollercoasters well anymore but maybe someone can convince you to try one of the many thrilling rides, else you will really miss out! You can do it!

LOCATION
RECOMMENDED ACTIVITIES

Enjoy one of the many thrilling rollercoasters and get some rest on the relaxing rides.

Take a walk in the park and enjoy the themed areas, foods and shows!

Go take a swim in the newly built waterpark RULANTICA right next to Europapark.

ROTHENBURG
[로텐부르크]
GENERAL

Rothenburg ob der Tauber is one of the best preserved medieval German towns. Walk through the small alleyways of its old-town and really immerse yourself in its rich history and captivating architecture. It also has a city wall just like Suwon! It's a really small town, you can easily see all of it within a day without being in a rush and still having time to sit down.

This city has a local sphere-shaped sweet (빵) called Schneeballen (means snowball). It is really delicious, you absolutely should try it. When I was here I ate one with chocolate, marzipan and Amaretto and was really happy with it.

While there is tourism as well, it's not too much to be annoying and you can enjoy it without much trouble.

LOCATION
RECOMMENDED ACTIVITIES

Enjoy some Glühwein (뱅쇼) at the Christmas Market and visit the German Christmas Museum.

Learn more about the history of Germany in the Medieval Crime Museum.

Take a walk on the old city walls and enjoy the view over the city from the tower.

HAMBURG
[함부르크]
GENERAL

Hamburg, located all the way in the north of Germany, is one of the largest and oldest port cities of Europe. You can see many beautiful old docks and warehouses at the port, as well as the newly built Elbphilarmonie. The Elbphilarmonie is a giant concert hall, and is among most expensive buildings in the world. It is truly and incredible sight! If you have a chance, you should listen to a concert in there, but you can also just go and enjoy the view from there.

The city is really well-known for it's vibrant nightlife at the famous Reeperbahn. While this is not a places to feel comfortable, it is worth a one-time visit. Walk through the streets and dive into a completely different world.

LOCATION
RECOMMENDED ACTIVITIES

Join a boat trip through Hamburg and take a glance at the massive harbour.

Watch a Disney musical at the Musical Boulevard.

Visit the Miniatur Wunderland, the world's largest model railway exhibition.

BODENSEE
[보덴제]
GENERAL

There are numerous beautiful lakeside towns around the Bodensee. You should definitely check out Konstanz, Bregenz and Friedrichshafen.

You can get the Bodensee Card Plus (76€ for 3 days), to enjoy countless activities around the lake. It offers boat trips, cable cars tickets and much more. So if you want to do a lot, instead of just taking a rest in the beautiful scenery, it should be really worth it.

The Bodensee is also really close to the mountains of the Alps. There are many great places to go hiking in the summer or snowboarding in the winter.

LOCATION
RECOMMENDED ACTIVITIES

Make a city tour around the Bodensee and visit cities in all three countries.

Try water sports like water skiing or stand-up paddling.

Here really is the perfect place to ride a bike. If you haven't learned it yet, now is the time!

KÖLN
[쾰른]
GENERAL

Köln is a beautiful city with the Rhein river flowing through it. The cathedral of Köln is really breathtaking. It has the third highest church towers in the whole world and you can even go up there and have a view over the city.

There is carnival all over Germany, but Köln has by far the most famous one, where people dress up in all kinds of different costumes. While there are different carnival events, the Rosenmontagszug is the most interesting one. It is a parade, where hundreds of customized people on themed vehicles drive through the streets and throw sweets at you. It's either in February or March every year. The atmosphere is really interesting.

LOCATION
RECOMMENDED ACTIVITIES

Watch the Rosenmontag carnival parade in Köln's downtown.

Take a stroll along the Rhein river to escape the noise of the city.

Visit the Lindt Chocolate Museum and participate in a chocolate tasting.

SHOPPING

German supermarkets are really awesome, there are a lot of different ones, but I would recommend you REWE and EDEKA. Most of German cities don't have any convenience stores, and supermarkets usually close around 8pm. So you can't really buy anything after that! Another thing is that ALL stores are closed on Sundays. So don't plan to go shopping on a Sunday!

By the way you can find more tips by pressing the icons at the bottom of the pop-up windows.


Enjoy your time in Germany and have an awesome holiday!

SAFETY

Another thing is that you should be really careful. Please don't walk outside alone at night, always be with a friend! And if there is no way around it and you have to be alone, be on the phone with someone. There is a hotline for that, you can reach them with this phone number +49 30-12074182. You can be on a call with them until you reach your accommodation. They will help you get home safely and it's completely for free. Please don't be shy to use it! Furthermore if you go to a bar, or something similar, never take a drink from a stranger or leave your drink unattended! They could add knockout drops to your drink, that make you lose your conciousness, so be careful.

TRANSPORTATION

Regarding transportation, there is a one-month-ticket for public transport, called Deutschland-Ticket. You can use the ticket for all means of public transport, like buses, subway and trains as much as you want. The only exception are the ICE/IC trains (they are like KTX and need extra tickets). Normal train tickets are really expensive and if you want to visit different cities, the Deutschland-Ticket is a great way to save money. You can buy it here. But be careful, the ticket is a subscription, so you should cancel it right after buying it for the month you want to travel.
